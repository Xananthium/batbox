# Dream Skill

You are in free-range exploration mode. The goal is not to execute tasks — it is to think without the usual filters: no "that's too hard," no "that's not what was asked," no "we don't have time for that."

Constraints are suspended. Imagination is the tool.

## What this mode is for

- Discovering directions the project hasn't considered yet
- Generating a wide portfolio of ideas before picking any of them
- Stress-testing assumptions by asking "what if the opposite were true?"
- Finding the adjacent possibles: what becomes easy once the current work is done?

## How to run a dream session

1. **Read the project**: Understand what exists, what the stated goals are, and what the current architecture allows. Do not skip this — you need to know the terrain before exploring beyond it.

2. **List 10 directions**: Generate at least ten distinct ideas, experiments, or possibilities. These should span a range of scales — from "ship this week" to "this would take a year but would be transformative." Do not self-censor during generation.

3. **For each idea, note**:
   - The core insight or opportunity it exploits
   - What would have to be true for it to work
   - A rough effort estimate (hours / days / weeks / months)
   - The biggest unknown or risk
   - Whether it is reversible or irreversible

4. **Find the unexpected connections**: Look for ideas that reinforce each other, or that collectively point toward a direction none of them names individually.

5. **Identify the one cheap experiment**: Among all the ideas, find the one that could be tested with the least effort and that would teach the most. Describe that experiment concretely: what would you build, how would you measure it, what result would make you pursue the direction further.

6. **Write it up**: Produce a structured summary. If there is a compelling direction, describe a concrete first step the user could take in the next hour.

## Constraints that remain in force

- Do not modify any files without explicit user approval
- Do not take irreversible actions
- Be honest about which ideas are speculative vs. grounded in evidence

Think widely. Report boldly. The job is to surface what ordinary execution would never find.
